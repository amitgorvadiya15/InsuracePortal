How’s your balance?

Finding a perfect balance between work and life would be great. But this balancing act can be close to impossible if you run your own business. In fact, it can often be hard to see where work ends and life begins, let alone carve out any leisure time. So company name, with the support of mental health charity Mind, are taking a good look at how the UK’s small business owners are coping.

Check out the results of our survey, take our quiz to see how you measure up, and explore our real-life tips to help you reach that blissful balance.

The Detail Obsessive

The quest for perfection gets you out of bed and your desk is immaculate. Misplaced apostrophes upset you, and you’re often told to chill out. For a bit of harmony, try a Japanese desk garden. You can draw lines in the sand with a ruler. Recommended for you: our article on ways of loosening up at work.

The Taskmaster

Some might call you domineering, but you know you're a visionary decision maker. What is the deal with these amateurs anyway? Throw out your exhausted stress ball and up your spinning classes for a jolt of everyday Zen. Recommended for you: our article on workplace relaxation methods.

The Yogi

Most often seen giving out free hugs and keeping everyone calm, decisions are your nemesis and you hate to rock the boat. Take some ‘you time’ and push the woes of others aside. Read a personal development book, such as The Art of Selfishness.

The Creative Whirlwind

Ideas and inspiration seep from your every pore. Being asked to calm it down and think inside the box raises your blood pressure. The word ‘compliance’ makes you pull weird faces. Regain your creative headspace by Googling Jackson Pollock. Recommended for you: our article on time management for creative types.

The big balance survey

Quizzes are great for a bit of fun, but to get a deeper look at business habits and working patterns we surveyed the UK’s business owners. This is just the start of what we found …

Almost 50% of business owners cancel events with family and friends during the week due to work pressures.

26% take less than 10 days off work annually, and over 5% don’t have time to take a holiday at all.

Over 60% of sole traders and small business owners say that work stops them getting a good night’s sleep.

Top tips for better balance

  1. #1 However busy things get, plan in some time each week for friends and family.
  2. #2 Build holiday time into your plans so you prepare for it well in advance.
  3. #3 Keep your mind and body going with exercise and healthy eating. There’s loads of advice out there, and it’ll help you and the business.
  4. #4 Wind down in the evening and get a good night’s sleep. It’s crucial for wellbeing and concentration.
  5. #5 Create a Zen workspace. If you need some inspiration, check out our workspace tips from leading home-based bloggers.
